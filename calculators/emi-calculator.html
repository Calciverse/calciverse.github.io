What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed monthly payment made by a borrower to repay a loan. It includes both the principal amount and the interest charged by the lender.

How EMI is Calculated?

The EMI amount depends on three main factors: loan amount, interest rate, and tenure. The calculation is based on a standard formula used by banks and financial institutions.

EMI Formula

EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N – 1]

Example Calculation

If you take a loan of ₹1,00,000 at 10% annual interest for 12 months, the EMI will be calculated using the above formula and result in fixed monthly payments.

FAQ

Is EMI fixed?
Yes, EMI remains fixed unless interest rate changes.

Can EMI be reduced?
Yes, by increasing tenure or reducing interest rate.
