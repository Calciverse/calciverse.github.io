What is Fixed Deposit (FD)?

A Fixed Deposit (FD) is a secure investment option offered by banks and financial institutions where you deposit a lump sum amount for a fixed period at a predetermined interest rate. FD investments are low-risk and ideal for conservative investors.

How FD Interest is Calculated?

FD maturity amount is calculated using the compound interest formula:

A = P (1 + r/n)nt

  • P = Principal amount
  • r = Annual interest rate
  • n = Compounding frequency per year
  • t = Investment tenure in years

Benefits of Fixed Deposit

  • Guaranteed returns
  • Low investment risk
  • Flexible tenure options
  • Suitable for short-term and long-term savings

FD vs Other Investment Options

FD is safer compared to mutual funds and stock investments. However, returns are lower compared to market-linked instruments. For higher growth, you can try our SIP Calculator or Compound Interest Calculator.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is FD better than savings account?
Yes, FD usually offers higher interest rates compared to savings accounts.

Can I withdraw FD before maturity?
Yes, but premature withdrawal may attract a penalty.

Is FD taxable?
Yes, interest earned on FD is taxable as per income tax rules.
